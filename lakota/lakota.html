The Sioux Nation is made up of seven groups collectively called Oceti Sakowin, or "Seven Council Fires." The seven groups identified themselves as Dakota, Nakota, or Lakota depending upon which of these three dialects they spoke. The largest group [of Lakota], the Teton, split into seven bands. The Lakota primarily live in South Dakota. from: South Dakota Historical Society
