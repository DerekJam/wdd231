Spotted Tail
Spotted Tail, Brulé man, half-length portrait, seated, facing slightly right. [between 1870 and 1881]
Spotted Tail (Sinte Galeska), a major Brule Sioux leader in the Plains Indian wars, was born to a man named Chunka (Tangled Hair) and a mother named Walks With Pipe, probably along the White River of South Dakota. Known as Jumping Buffalo in his youth, Spotted Tail got his adult name from a striped raccoon pelt that was given to him by a trapper.

from: Encyclopedia of the Great Plains/University of Nebraska-Lincoln website.


Spotted Tail was born in South Dakota in 1823, and passed away on the Rosebud Indian reservation, South Dakota, on August 5, 1881.
To learn more about Spotted Tail, please also visit: Aktá Lakota Museum & Cultural Center website.
